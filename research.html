Research introduction

Applied econometrics for policy and market design questions.

My research interests are applied econometrics, causal inference, policy evaluation, and high-dimensional econometrics. I am especially interested in empirical designs that connect credible identification with real policy and market settings.

Research themes

Current directions

Urban taxi markets

Studying how surcharges affect passenger and driver behavior in NYC Yellow Cab markets, with attention to asymmetric and distinct surcharge effects.

Macroeconomic announcements

Measuring whether recession announcements matter, including limited average effects and state-dependent responses.

High-dimensional forecasting

Using high-dimensional predictors, factor models, and machine learning methods for nowcasting and financial volatility forecasting.
